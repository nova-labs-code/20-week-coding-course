Week 2: HTML Intermediate

Welcome to Week 2! This week we focus on semantic structure, grouping elements, colors, lists with links/images, tables, and basic forms. Each day uses the “Learn → See → Do → Check” method and should take about 30 minutes.

Day 1: Semantic HTML & Sections

Learn: Semantic HTML uses tags that give meaning to content, like <header>, <footer>, <main>, <section>, and <article>.

<header><h1>My Website</h1></header>
<main>
  <section><h2>About Me</h2><p>Content here.</p></section>
</main>
<footer><p>Copyright 2026</p></footer>

Do: Reorganize your Week 1 profile using <header> for your name, <main> for content, and <section> for About Me and Hobbies.

Check: Open in a browser to verify structure and readability.

Day 2: Divs & Spans

Learn: <div> and <span> are non-semantic containers for styling or scripting; <div> is block-level, <span> is inline.

<div class="bio"><p>This is a paragraph</p></div>
<p>I like <span style="color:red;">coding</span>!</p>

Do: Wrap your paragraphs in <div> and highlight a word with <span> in a different color.

Check: Open in a browser to verify grouping and inline styles.

Day 3: Colors & Backgrounds

Learn: Inline styles can change text color and backgrounds, e.g., <p style="color:blue;">Blue text</p> or <div style="background-color:#f0f0f0;">Section</div>.

Do: Change text colors, section backgrounds, and highlight areas of your profile page.

Check: Open in a browser to see how colors affect readability and appeal.

Day 4: Lists with Links & Images

Learn: Lists can include links and images:

<ul>
  <li><a href="https://example.com">My Site</a></li>
  <li><img src="icon.png" alt="Icon"></li>
</ul>

Do: Update your hobbies list to add a small image or icon for each hobby and make one item link to an external site.

Check: Open in a browser to verify images and links display correctly.

Day 5: Tables

Learn: Tables organize data with <table>, <tr> for rows, <th> for headers, <td> for cells.

<table>
  <tr><th>Day</th><th>Task</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Monday</td><td>Code</td></tr>
</table>

Do: Create a table of your weekly routine with at least three rows.

Check: Open in a browser and confirm the table displays correctly with proper alignment.

Day 6: Forms (Basic Inputs)

Learn: Forms collect input using <form> with <input> and <textarea>.

<form>
  <label>Name:</label><input type="text" placeholder="Your Name">
  <label>Message:</label><textarea placeholder="Type here"></textarea>
  <input type="submit">
</form>

Do: Add a contact form with Name, Email, and Message fields plus a submit button.

Check: Open in a browser to ensure fields are interactive.

Day 7: Review & Mini Intermediate Page

Learn: Combine all skills from Week 2 to enhance your Week 1 profile page.

Do: Use <header>, <main>, <div>, colors, lists with links/images, a table, and a simple contact form.

Check: Open in a browser to ensure layout is logical, colors are readable, links work, images display, table aligns, and form inputs are interactive.
