Week 16: User Experience & Design

This week focuses on **creating a smooth, user-friendly website experience**, making sure your visitors can navigate, understand, and enjoy your content.

Day 1: Navigation Design

Learn: Menus and navigation paths guide users. Clear labels, logical page order, and visible menus improve usability. Do: Sketch your main menu with all top-level pages and potential submenus. Check: Ensure users can reach any page in 2–3 clicks or less.

Day 2: Mobile-First Thinking

Learn: Many users browse on mobile. Design for small screens first, then scale up. Do: Draw your homepage layout as it would appear on a phone, focusing on readable text and tappable buttons. Check: Layout remains clear without horizontal scrolling and text is legible.

Day 3: Accessibility

Learn: Make your site usable for all. Check color contrast, readable fonts, alt text for images, and keyboard navigation. Do: Test your page with high-contrast mode, tab navigation, and screen reader tools. Check: All content is accessible without relying only on color or mouse.

Day 4: Visual Hierarchy

Learn: Guide users’ attention with headings, spacing, images, and highlights. Do: Organize a sample page using headings, paragraphs, and images so the most important info stands out. Check: Someone unfamiliar should understand the page quickly.

Day 5: User Flow Testing

Learn: Simulate users completing tasks like “Sign up” or “Find a blog post.” Look for friction points. Do: Ask a friend to navigate your site mockup and note confusion points. Check: Identify at least 2 areas to improve usability.

Day 6: Feedback Systems

Learn: Collect user feedback through forms, polls, or comment sections. Do: Create a feedback form asking 2–3 questions about your content and layout. Check: Form should be easy to fill and submit, and responses readable.

Day 7: Weekly Review

Learn: Combine insights from navigation, mobile design, accessibility, hierarchy, user flow, and feedback. Do: Update your site mockup or prototype with improvements. Check: You should have a refined, user-friendly design ready for content and eventual launch.
